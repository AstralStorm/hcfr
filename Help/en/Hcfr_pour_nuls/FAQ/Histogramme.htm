    1. In which order should I operate to optimize my settings ?

      1 -       CIE chart with measurement of the primary colors only. This allows checking the coherence of the chart.

      2 - Measurement of the grey scale while adjusting the gain-offset settings of the projector until a correct result is obtained..

      3 - Adjustment of the gamma curve (brightness histogram)

      4 - Measurement of the secondary colors (which depend on the grey scale's settings)

      5 - Measurement of the on/off contrast and the ANSI contrast (this last one may need changing probe's position).

       

    2. To what the Delta E curve (in purple in our example) from the RGB histogram is related ?

      The Delta E curve (from RGB graph) measures the distance from a white reference (D65) in the color space. Ideally, the distance should always be under 3, but it is seldom an easy task.

      The most important is the linearity of these curves. You should keep in mind the calibration itself is not perfect, so a 0 is not a true 0 and could be a 3 or even a 6.



      In theory, the 3 RGB curves should be the most linear as possible on the entire gray scale (from 0 to 100 IRE).

      Then, they should be ideally overlaped.
       

    3. I do not see any detail in dark areas ?

      Most of the time, the lack of detail in dark areas is related to a wrong gamma adjustment in the low IRE levels (from 0 to 30). Thus, the important thing for these IRE levels is to obtain a linear adjustment of the gamma as close as possible to the reference value (2.2) and in a linear way.
