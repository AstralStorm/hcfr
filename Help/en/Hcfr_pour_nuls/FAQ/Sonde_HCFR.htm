    1. Positioning of the probe.

      In general vis-a-vis the screen.

      But that depends on several parameters!

      Mainly type of projector and sensibility of the sensor. If you do not have results satisfying in the 0, 10. 30 IRE, it is that the sensor seized not weak the level, either because of its intrinsic limits (not very probable), or because of the environment (luminous reflexions, etc..) is because of the too weak projector, screen. etc

      Best is to use it vis-a-vis the screen (and thus to take account of the possible impact of the screen) but it is sometimes not possible (with the TT with weak luminosity for example.).

      The probe must be placed at a distance ranging between       0.20 m and 1 m vis-a-vis the screen. Determination of the good value being able to be done only after experimentation.

      In order to ensure a repetitively       of measurements it is of primary importance that the probe is always placed at the same place.

      For the measurement of       Contrast Ratio (CR), the probe must be turned towards the projector (in the case of use of the probe for a projector) If the probe is placed vis-a-vis the projector it is necessary to position a diffuser on the sensor.


       

    2. Diffuser.  

      With a matric projector, we have the pixels, and the inter-pixels. Like sensors are tiny, we can have placed them very well on a inter-pixel, and not to measure a realistic value, from where the need to diffuse the light.  Vis-a-vis the screen, like this last diffuses the light, not need for diffuser on sensors.
       

    3. Use the sensor to collect IR codes.

      The colorimeter is inapt for such a use. Indeed, to collect the codes IR, it a true receiver IR is necessary. The sensors of the colorimeter are not made for this use, and cruelly miss sensitivity to receive a code IR and to interpret it.       One can do it, but in the black, with the remote control with a few centimeters.
