Displayed points
 

Display measures points

You can control which points are displayed using contextual menu. The three first options let you display or not points corresponding to gray scale, saturations, and free measures. Primary colors and Secondaries are always displayed, and the color selected in measure grid too.
 

 

Coordinates are represented in CIE xy units. Those coordinates are visible directly in the measures view, when display unit is « xyz » (no caps !) ou « xyY ». The xy coordinates in CIE diagram are values displayed on x and y lines in those two display units.

Secondary colors, when measured, also appear on the graph. Sometimes, some projectors offer settings concerning secondaries. What is important is to examine the dotted lines between each secondary color and opposite primary color on gamut triangle. Idealy, those three lines should cut in a unique point (and not draw a small central triangle), and the crossing point should be exactly D65. D65 point is shown in reference points, but also by two vertical and horizontal dotted lines which mark its xy coordinates on CIE diagram (0.313, 0.329). If intersection point is too far from D65, you can try a new setting for color temperature, which can move secondaries.
 

  1. Display grays 

    The points are the different gray levels (percent or IRE) measured on gray scale. Idealy, they should all converge on D65. More they are far from D65, more their Delta E grows.
     

  2. Display saturations 

    Saturation measures can be drawn on DIE diagram. They must be regularly spaced on a line from D65 to the primary or secondary saturated color. Saturation setting evaluation is easier when using     Saturation – Luminance and Saturation – Shifts.
     

  3. Display free measures 

    This options displays points marching free measures. Only 20 last points are shown, to avoid making the diagram unreadable.

 

When primary colors have been measured, the projector gamut triangle appears. Ideally, the reference colorspace gamut should be entirely inside projector gamut triangle, to guarantee that it can display the whole standard colorspace.

 

The fourth option allow displaying primary and secondary color points from the reference document. Those points are displayed as small stars and a gamut triangle, over document points, which allow easy comparisons between them.
