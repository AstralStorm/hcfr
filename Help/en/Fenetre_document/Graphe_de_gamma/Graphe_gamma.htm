Generalities

 

Gamma graph

The gamma graph display gamma values computed from Gray Scale measures.

X axis shows gray levels, in percentage or IRE levels, with as many points than there are measures. On Y axis, the luminance is expressed in logarithmic value, relative to maximum luminance, obtained at 100% gray (or 100 IRE). Points at 0 and 100 are not computable.

During calibration, one of the goals is to approach as much as possible the reference curve, which must be perfectly flat, using projector settings. For this purpose, you use the black level, white level and gamma settings.

Gamma values recommended varies slightly in literature. The 2.2 value match natural response of a CRT tube, upon video standards are based. This is the default value in HCFR Colorimeter, but you can change it as you like. With a high contrast projector used in a very dark room, you can target a higher gamma, probably up to 2.5. It's partially a question of taste, but also of using conditions. Higher the gamma, higher the perceived contrast, but the overall luminosity decreases, and black level details become less visible. At the opposite, a low gamma allow a high readability of dark details, a higher global luminosity, but the overall image looks poorly contrasted, and can look veiled.
 


 

The options relative to this graph are available using right mouse button. They can define components, reference curves, define scale, graph parameters, and save graph in a file.

This graph may display additional information relative to luxmeter measures, when it is present. For details about luxmeter use, see advanced preferences.
