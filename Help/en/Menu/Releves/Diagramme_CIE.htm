CIE Diagram

The CIE diagram makes it possible to post the points of the grey scale with various useful references.

CIE diagram is a graphic representation in the shape of wing which represents the location of all visible wavelengths.

The pure or completely saturated colors are on the contour of the spectrum and is at 100%.

The red is located in the corner lower right, the green with the higher point and blue in the left lower corner of the diagram.

The straight line which connects the two ends of the spectrum is called “the line of crimsons " . One can locate the place of the black body in the central zone, called “source”.

The curve represents the color of a theoretical black body when the temperature rises at 1000K ad infinitum.

Point A is the color of a bulb with tungsten filament filled with gas (T = 2856K).

The source B represents the sun with the meridian. C represents a covered sky at midday (white of reference of television).

The point C represents a sky covered at midday (white of reference of television).

The source D (famous D65) correspondent with a black body radiating with T = 6504K is a white and a little greener which is used as reference regarding video calibration.
