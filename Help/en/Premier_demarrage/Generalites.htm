Préliminary

It is important to understand that the calibration requires to know basic concepts of Video, for understanding well after a measurement what it occurs to the image.

To have experience is inevitably more appreciable.

You have the tool, the remainder, it is patience, and a little bit of effort. 

 

Stage N°1 :
 

  • Validation of the positioning of the probe  

    First, place the probe front of the screen, at approximately 40-50 centimeters (15 to 20 inches). Then, throw a series of measurements of the primary colors .

    Look at the graph which presents CIE triangle to us, in order to ensure itself that the triangle calculated starting from measurements obtained is acceptable: the white triangle is close in its form with the theoretical black triangle. If the triangle is minimalist or although errors of additivity are met, useless to go further.

    Once correct triangle CIE, one can pass at the stage     n° 2.

 

Stage N°2 :
 

  • Adjustment of Gamma (histogram of brightness).  

    Then, the second stage is to obtain a correct curve of gamma. Only after seek to have a temperature curve to 6500K on the grey scale.

    To start, what is a correct gamma? The objective is to have the best dynamic image possible, on the whole of the grey scale (from 0 to 100 IRE), to have in the bottom of the spectrum (from 0 to 30 IRE) of the emerged blacks and in the top of the spectrum (from 70 to 100 IRE) to have white which are not burned.

    “    HCFR Colorimeter” allows to read the result of an adjustment of gamma. One must obtain an almost linear line of 0 to 100 IRE, with a value near the standard, that is to say 2.2.

    According to the type of technology employed by the diffuser (LCD, CRT, DLP, SXRD, D-ILA, etc), gamma will be easier to regulate without losing too much on the level of the quality of the blacks.

    If towards 90 IRE, the curve falls to the bottom (sign of whites burned), this is never dramatic with the image.

For recall if need be:

Blacks blocked = few details in the dark scenes = value of gamma > 2.5 from 0 to 40 IRE (approximately)
Blacks which draws on the “gray” = black not very dense = much details in the dark scenes = value from gamma < 2     from 0 to 40 IRE (approximately)
White burned = details crushed, absent, gummed in the strong luminosities = value of gamma < 2.0 from 70 to 100 IRE (approx.)

The first of the things is of launching a measurement of the grey scale (in 10 points), then to analyze the result by posting the histogram of brightness (curve of gamma), and this in logarithmic curve mode. Then, to make so that the curve becomes a regular line, of 0 with 100 IRE. For that, I operate for my part on 3 levers:
 

      1. Adjustment of values of luminosity and contrast general. 

        Adjustment of values of luminosity and contrast general.

        These adjustments respectively operate the balance of the blacks and the balance of the white. If the luminosity is pushed, one gains in dynamic, intensity, and one emerges the image. But it is with the detriment of the quality of the blacks, that one “wheatens”.

        If contrast is lowered, one gains in quality of the blacks, but the image becomes dark pane and one tends to stop the blacks. Therefore, a balance to be found. To carry out tests to feel how behaves the diffuser. After a measurement, on the histogram of brightness and in posting log mode, if the curve “plunges down” towards 70 IRE, it is possible that the value of starting contrast is too high. If the curve leaves to 0 IRE very high (> 2.5), it is possible that the luminosity is already on a too strong value.
         

      2. Increase or reduction.....

        Increase or reduction (according to what one seeks to correct) of the values of the contrast of primary colors RGB or values of luminosity of primary colors RGB.

        It is necessary that the adjustments of the diffuser or the source allow it of course For example, if with the measurement of “        HCFR Colorimeter” I start with a gamma value near 2.8, but that the remainder is not badly, I can seek to lower the RED, GREEN luminosity and BLUE of some points on my projector, then I remake a series of measurements of the grey scale to validate the result, and so on.
         

      3. Post measurements

        Use (so available) of adjustments of gamma on the diffuser (high gamma, average gamma, low gamma for example on Panasonic PTAE700). May I use these adjustments only if I am there constrained (a bump or a hollow).
